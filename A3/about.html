About the Tale

Rapunzel is a fairy tale that dates back to the 17th century. It was popularized by the Brothers Grimm in the 1800s. It has since been adapted into films, plays, and stories worldwide, most famously Disney's "Tangled".

The tale has gone through many versions, but the core remains the same: a young girl locked in a tower, a prince, a witch, and the journey to freedom.

Fun Facts

  • The name "Rapunzel" comes from a plant known as rampion.
  • Her hair is often described as being over 70 feet long in modern adaptations.
  • It's one of the most illustrated and retold fairy tales in history.
